HVL was inviting submissions for internal funding for *excellent research environments*, to the tune of 3 years with 2 mio. NOK per year, which basically comes out to a post-doc plus decent overhead.

Surprisingly (not saying that we weren't excellent already, but you know that also a good amount of luck is always involved), our proposal themed "Towards an Excellent Research Environment in Trustworthy GenAI-assisted Software Engineering: Evidence, Methods, Educational and Industrial Impact (TERSE)" went through, and, maybe unsurprisingly, it'll involve some ✨AI✨ within software engineering.

> The objective of this proposal is to evolve HVL’s existing and already strong Software Engineering (SE) research group into an excellent and internationally visible research environment within trustworthy GenAI-assisted software engineering. The proposal exploits and builds upon our current strategic research strengths within empirical software engineering, model-based software engineering, and formal methods for software verification. The expected impact within software engineering is: (1) empirical evidence on the real-world effects and risks of GenAI; (2) actionable methods and guidelines for industry and public-sector; (3) up-to-date research and profession-oriented education at HVL; (4) a strong regional industry collaboration network; and (5) a step-change in the capacity of the research environment to win and be primary investigator in major externally funded projects (NFR/Horizon Europe/ERC).

Key-members are Lars Michael Kristensen (who also rode herd on the proposal process), Patrick Stünkel, Violet Ka I Pun, Rogardt Heldal and Volker Stolz as further WP-leads.
